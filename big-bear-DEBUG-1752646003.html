⚠️ Critical Safety Information - 6,750 Feet Elevation

  • UV Exposure: 40-68% higher than sea level - Use SPF 50+ sunscreen
  • Dehydration Risk: Drink 3-4 quarts water daily (25% higher than normal)
  • Cold Water: Lake temperature 61-75°F - hypothermia risk, constant supervision required
  • Altitude Effects: Some may experience headaches, fatigue - take it easy first day
  • Air Quality: Currently "Very Unhealthy" due to wildfire smoke - monitor conditions

🚨 Emergency Scenarios & Solutions

What to Do When Things Go Wrong in Big Bear Lake

Common Emergencies & Quick Solutions

⛰️ Altitude Sickness (Very Common!)

  • Symptoms: Headache, nausea, fatigue, dizziness
  • Immediate Action:
    • Rest and hydrate (no alcohol)
    • Take ibuprofen or acetaminophen
    • Eat carbs (helps with oxygen)
    • If severe: Descend to lower elevation
  • Prevention: First day take it easy, drink 3L water
  • Oxygen Available: Bear Valley Pharmacy rents O2 tanks

🐻 Bear Encounter at Cabin

  • If bear is outside:
    • Stay inside, make loud noises
    • Bang pots, yell, car alarm
    • Call: (909) 866-7232
    • Never feed or approach
  • If bear enters cabin:
    • Give it escape route
    • Get everyone upstairs/bathroom
    • Call 911 immediately
    • Make noise from safe location

🚤 Boat Emergency on Lake

  • Engine dies: Drop anchor, call marina, wave for help
  • Someone falls overboard: Throw cushion, circle back slowly, call 911
  • Storm approaching: Head to nearest shore immediately
  • Lake Patrol: (909) 866-2227 or Channel 16
  • All marinas monitor: VHF Channel 16

👶 Lost Child Protocols

  • Immediate: One parent stays at last seen spot
  • Alert: Nearest employee/lifeguard
  • Village/Store: They'll lock down and announce
  • Call 911: After 10 minutes
  • Prevention: Bright clothes, write phone # on arm
  • Meeting spot: Establish for each location
